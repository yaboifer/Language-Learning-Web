French for Beginners

Welcome to French for Beginners!

This webpage is designed for beginners who want to learn essential French phrases. You will find common phrases with audio that you can practice. Click the audio buttons to hear the correct pronunciation!

Common Phrases:

1. Bonjour - Hello / Good day (formal/informal)

2. Merci - Thank you

3. Au revoir - Goodbye

4. Je suis désolé - I am sorry

5. Oui - Yes

6. Non - No

7. Bonsoir - Good evening

8. Enhante - Nice to meet you

9. Je m'appelle - My name is...

10. Je ne comprends pas - I don' t understand

Pronunciation Tips:

Learning how to pronounce French words can feel tricky at first, but with practice and some tips you'll start sounding more natural in no time.

  • R sounds: The 'r' in french comes from the back of the throat, rather than the front like in English pronunciation.

  • Silent Letters Final consonants are often silent: petit, grand, fils.

  • Nasals (on, an, in, un, en) Don't pronounce the “n” fully—let the sound go through your nose.

    • "on" like in bon → nasalized "aw"
    • "an" / "en" like in maman → nasal "ah"
    • "in" / "ain" / "un" like in vin or pain → nasal "eh" or "uh"
