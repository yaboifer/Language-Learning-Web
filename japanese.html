Japanese for Beginners

Welcome to Japanese for Beginners!

This webpage is designed for beginners who want to learn essential Japanese phrases. You will find common phrases with audio that you can practice. Click the audio buttons to hear the correct pronunciation!

Common Phrases:

1. こんにちは (Konnichiwa) - Hello / Good afternoon

2. お元気ですか? (Ogenki desu ka?) - How are you?

3. ありがとう (Arigatou) - Thank you

4. おはようございます (Ohayou gozaimasu) - Good morning

5. さようなら (Sayounara) - Goodbye

6. お願いします (Onegaishimasu) - Please

7. はじめまして (Hajimemashite) - Nice to meet you

8. ありがとうございます (Arigatou gozaimasu) - Thank you very much

9. すみません (Sumimasen) - Excuse me / I'm sorry

10. トイレはどこですか? (Toire wa doko desu ka?) - Where is the bathroom?

Pronunciation Tips:

Japanese pronunciation is relatively straightforward, with each syllable being pronounced clearly. Here are some tips:

  • Vowels: In Japanese, vowels are pronounced as follows

  • あ (a) = "ah"
  • い (i) = "ee"
  • う (u) = "oo"
  • え (e) = "eh"
  • お (o) = "oh"

  • Double consonants (e.g., っ in こんにちは) are pronounced with a slight pause before the next sound.

  • The "r" sound is softer and rolled in Japanese, similar to a combination of an "l" and "r".

  • Pitch accent: Japanese has pitch accent, meaning the tone of a syllable can change the meaning of the word. For example, はし (hashi) with a high tone means "chopsticks," while with a low tone, it means "bridge".
